1. The "Command Center" (Dashboard)
This is the first screen the client sees. It needs to tell them the health of their business at a glance.

Live Status: How many courts/turfs are currently occupied vs. free.

Todayâ€™s Metrics: Total Bookings, Total Revenue, Check-ins pending.

Quick Actions: "New Booking," "Block Slot," "Add Expense."

Visual Graphs: Weekly revenue trend, Peak hours visualization (Heatmap).

2. Booking Management (The Core)
The heart of the system. It needs to be fast and intuitive for receptionists.

Interactive Calendar: A drag-and-drop calendar view (Day/Week/Month view) showing all courts/turfs side-by-side.

Booking Status: Color-coded slots (Confirmed, Pending Payment, Cancelled, Checked-In, Completed).

Slot Blocking: Ability to manually block slots for maintenance or private events.

Walk-in Booking: A quick form to book a slot for a customer standing at the desk (collect cash/UPI).

Reschedule/Cancel: Logic to handle refunds or wallet credits if a booking is moved.

Historical Data: Filter bookings by Date Range, Sport, or Specific Court to see past records.

3. Financials & Settlements (Crucial for You)
This addresses your specific requirement about payments and settlements.

Transaction Logs: A master list of every payment received from customers (Cash, UPI, Credit Card, Gateway).

Invoice Generation: Auto-generate PDF receipts for customers.

Expense Tracker: Allow the venue to log their own costs (Electricity, Staff salaries) to see net profit.

Platform Settlements (The "Pay You" Feature):

Commission Calculator: Auto-calculate how much they owe you (e.g., 5% per booking or a monthly subscription).

Wallet/Ledger: A digital ledger showing "Total Earnings," "Amount Settled," and "Amount Due to Platform."

Payment Gateway: A "Pay Now" button allowing the venue owner to transfer the due amount to you directly via the dashboard.

Settlement History: A history of payouts they have made to you with downloadable invoices.

4. Customer Relationship Management (CRM)
"Who is the customer?"

Customer Database: List of all users who have ever booked.

Customer Profile: Click on a name to see:

Contact details (Phone/Email).

Booking history (Total spend, favorite sport, frequency).

No-show history (Reliability score).

Membership/Subscriptions: Management of monthly passes or loyalty points for regular players.

Blacklist: Ability to ban abusive customers from making future bookings.

5. Tournament & League Management
Since sports venues often host leagues, this is a high-value feature.

Tournament Setup: Create a tournament page with entry fees and max teams.

Team Registration: Manage team names and player lists.

Fixture Scheduling: Assign matches to specific courts/times automatically.

Scoreboard Entry: Admin can update scores which reflect on the user-side app (if you have one).

6. Venue & Inventory Settings
Court/Turf Management: Add new courts, rename them, and set specific pricing for each.

Dynamic Pricing:

Peak vs. Off-Peak: Set higher prices for weekends/evenings.

Slot Duration: Prices for 60 mins vs 90 mins.

Inventory/Equipment: If they rent out bats, balls, or shoes, a simple tracker to manage stock and rental fees.

7. Staff & Permissions (RBAC)
Role Management: The "Owner" sees everything. The "Receptionist" might only see Bookings and not the Total Revenue or Settlement data.

Shift Management: Track which staff member booked which slot (for accountability).

8. Analytics & Reports
Occupancy Reports: Which days are dead? Which hours are busiest?

Revenue Reports: Monthly/Yearly financial breakdown.

Customer Retention: How many new vs. returning customers.
